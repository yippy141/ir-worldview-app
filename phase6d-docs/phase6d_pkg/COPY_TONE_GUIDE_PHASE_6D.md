# Copy Tone Guide — Phase 6D

## Goal

Make visible result/profile/atlas copy sound:
- clear
- human
- editorial
- serious but not academic
- natural in rhythm

## Prefer
- `You usually...`
- `You tend to...`
- `You are more likely to...`
- `You often start with...`
- `This pattern usually...`
- `In practice, this often leads to...`
- `What this can look like in real debates...`

## Avoid as recurring phrasing
- `X matters`
- `You think X matters`
- `nearest-fit shorthand`
- `issue read, not a mini-identity`
- `case by case`
- `conditional` repeated multiple times in the same section
- `mixed normative style`
- `broad-spectrum baseline`
- long noun clusters like `coalition-durability logic`

## Sentence rules
- aim for shorter first sentences
- visible summaries should usually be 1 to 2 sentences, not 4
- avoid two hedges in one sentence
- avoid writing that sounds like the model is explaining its own uncertainty before saying anything useful

## Card rules
Atlas cards should be readable in about 5 seconds.
That means:
- one clear sentence
- 2 to 3 short driver tags
- one short pressure note

## Detail-page rules
Detail pages may be more interpretive, but the first screen should still answer:
- what this pattern usually emphasizes
- what it often underestimates
- how it behaves under pressure

## Rewrite patterns

### Weak
`This pattern keeps several neighboring arguments in play.`

### Better
`You do not collapse into one school easily. Several neighboring instincts stay live at the same time.`

### Weak
`This pattern keeps open the possibility that severe moral stakes can outweigh strict non-intervention.`

### Better
`You do not treat sovereignty as absolute. In extreme cases, moral harm can outweigh strict non-intervention.`

### Weak
`This pattern does not settle into one clean doctrine.`

### Better
`Your answers pull in different directions across domains, so no single doctrine captures them cleanly.`

### Weak
`Technology read: selective control, case by case.`

### Better
`You favor targeted controls over blanket openness or blanket restriction.`

## Voice target
Closer to:
- strong policy-editor prose
- a sharp explain-it-to-an-intelligent-friend tone
- your natural voice when you write plainly and directly

Further from:
- taxonomy-first explainers
- academic abstract language
- AI-safe hedging language
- pseudo-scientific personality-test voice
