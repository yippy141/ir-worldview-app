Use this as source material, not as sacred text. The agent should adapt copy to existing components and layout.

## Product promise

### Preferred hero

**How do you read world politics?**

Work through foreign-policy dilemmas and get a personal briefing on the arguments you trust, the tradeoffs you accept, and the places your instincts split.

This is not a personality test or a claim about your true ideology. It is a structured way to inspect how you reason when power, rules, risk, legitimacy, markets, and technology collide.

Primary CTA: **Start the Foundation**  
Secondary CTA: **Try AI Governance**  
Tertiary CTA: **Explore the field guide**

### Shorter hero

**Map how you think about power, risk, and cooperation.**

Answer scenario-based questions and get a briefing on your foreign-policy instincts: where they are consistent, where they split, and which arguments you tend to trust.

## Three-part architecture

**Foundation** gives you the baseline.  
It maps the arguments you return to across power, institutions, domestic politics, legitimacy, markets, restraint, order, and justice.

**Focus-area modules** test the baseline.  
Security and Technology show whether your instincts harden, soften, or split when the case becomes concrete.

**Profile** keeps the pieces together.  
It brings saved results into one place without pretending they form a single master score.

## AI Governance bridge

**Here for AI policy? Start with the AI Governance Compass.**

It stands on its own as a read on frontier-AI governance instincts. The IR Foundation adds depth if you want to see how those instincts connect to your wider view of power, cooperation, technology, and risk.

### Better pattern

**Rules when they bite; leverage when they do not.**

Your answers lean institutionalist, but not because you trust institutions automatically. You seem to trust rules when they monitor behavior, make cheating costly, or help coalitions hold together. When rules look symbolic, you shift toward bargaining power.

- Closest tradition: Liberal Institutionalism
- Secondary pull: Strategic Realism
- Style: cautious coalition-builder
- Main tension: rules vs leverage

## Research opt-in copy

**Help improve the inventory**

You can use, save, and share this result without contributing it to a research dataset.

In a later beta, you may choose to add a de-identified or pseudonymous copy of your result and answers to help test question quality and produce aggregate insight reports. This build does not send your answers anywhere from this block.

No ads. No sale of profile data. No political targeting. Optional contact details should stay separate from answer data.

## Words and phrases to cut

- “shapes your instincts” when it implies a single hidden identity
- “layer” when “result,” “module,” or “section” would be clearer
- “overlay” in user-facing copy unless defined
- “surface patterns”
- “multidimensional orientation”
- “strategic disposition”
- “where useful” without saying useful for what
- “real emphasis”
- “X matters” as a sentence pattern
- “in many ways,” “broadly speaking,” “complex,” “nuanced,” “dynamic”

## Method caveat for result pages

This result reflects patterns across your scenario judgments. It is a structured interpretation, not a diagnosis, identity claim, or validated psychometric score.
