# V13 beta testing plan

## Goal
Run a small external beta with enough structure to learn, but without pretending the product is validated.

## Recommended beta size
Start with 10–20 people.

Segments to include:
- 3–5 IR / policy people
- 3–5 AI governance / safety adjacent people
- 3–5 smart non-specialist friends
- 2–3 undergraduate / early-career users
- 1–2 corporate / strategy people if available

## Test paths
Ask people to choose one path:

### Path A — Quick baseline
Foundation only, then result.

### Path B — Baseline + one pressure test
Foundation + one module + Profile.

### Path C — AI-first
AI Governance Compass + AI Atlas / Field Guide.

### Path D — Full worldview mosaic
Foundation + one module + AI + Profile.

## Invite copy
I’m testing a beta version of an IR / AI worldview inventory I built as a serious editorial interactive, not a validated personality test. It maps a baseline foreign-policy worldview, optional issue modules, and an AI governance layer into a profile.

It should take 10–20 minutes depending on how much you explore. I’m especially interested in whether the result feels clear, useful, and honest — and where you get bored or confused.

Research response storage is optional. You can use the product without opting in.

## Feedback questions
Ask these after they test:

1. What path did you take? Foundation only, module, AI, or Profile?
2. Where did you first feel interested?
3. Where did you first feel bored or overloaded?
4. Did your result feel like a useful interpretation or too abstract?
5. Did Profile explain what changed after modules/AI?
6. Which sentence felt most personally accurate?
7. Which sentence sounded most AI-written or unnatural?
8. Did the data opt-in feel clear and non-creepy?
9. Would you share this with someone else? Who?
10. What is the one thing you would cut or simplify?

## What to measure
Use product analytics for:
- route starts
- route completions
- Profile opens
- Atlas / Field Guide opens
- share-link copy events

Use opt-in research storage for:
- raw answer distributions
- result distributions
- module/AI interactions
- question drift analysis

## What to publish later
Safe aggregate posts:
- “What early testers disagreed about most”
- “Which IR baseline shifted most under AI governance questions”
- “Where policy people and non-specialists diverged”
- “Questions that produced the most hesitation”

Do not publish:
- small-N identifiable slices
- raw individual profiles
- employer-level or named-community results without explicit consent
