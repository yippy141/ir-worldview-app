# V14 pilot share plan

## Goal
Get useful feedback without overexposing the project before the product loop is stable.

## Audience split

### AI governance / safety / tech policy contacts
Send them directly to `/ai`.

Ask them:
1. Did the AI Compass feel like it mapped live governance disagreements?
2. Did any axis feel too collapsed or too Western-centric?
3. Was your result interesting enough to share or discuss?
4. What source, debate, or perspective felt missing?
5. Would this help structure a discussion with colleagues?

### SAIS / IR / policy-school contacts
Send them to `/` or `/quiz`.

Ask them:
1. Did you know where to start?
2. Did the Foundation result feel complete on its own?
3. Did the result card say something more useful than a label?
4. Did the module/AI/Profile relationship make sense?
5. What felt too academic, too obvious, or too long?

### Engineer / builder contacts
Send them the live preview and GitHub branch.

Ask them:
1. Did anything feel like vibe-coded slop?
2. Did the routes, share links, and mobile behavior work?
3. Did the README and methods notes match what the product actually does?
4. Was any data/consent behavior unclear?
5. What would you fix before showing this to employers?

## Pilot size
Start with 10 to 20 people:
- 5 IR/SAIS people
- 5 AI governance / tech policy people
- 3 builder/engineer people
- 2 to 7 smart generalists

## Do not do yet
- Do not post broadly on LinkedIn until at least 5 people finish.
- Do not ask for too much feedback in one form.
- Do not ask people to take every module.
- Do not overreact to one person's idiosyncratic complaint.

## Success criteria
Proceed to broader LinkedIn post if:
- at least 70% of pilot users finish the path you sent them
- fewer than 2 people call the result generic or obvious
- no one reports broken share links
- no one is creeped out by data opt-in
- at least 3 people say they would share or discuss their result
