# IR + AI V12 — Foundation hardening memo

This memo is for the limited question / methods pass only.

## Scope rule

Touch only a short named set of Foundation stems.
Preserve:
- question IDs
- polarity
- scoring behavior
- route behavior

## Why these items are the priority

These are the ones most likely to feel either too agreeable, too broad, or too close to common-sense truism rather than real discrimination.

## Targeted stem groups

### 1. Security rivalry stem

Risk:
- easy for many thoughtful users to agree with
- can read as a generic truth instead of a real contrast

Direction:
- make the disagreement about whether rivalry is the **main organizing logic** or one factor among several

### 2. Institutions stem

Risk:
- too easy to endorse as modest common sense

Direction:
- sharpen the contrast between institutions changing incentives versus merely organizing coordination among actors whose underlying interests remain unchanged

### 3. Domestic politics stem

Risk:
- broad enough that many users will endorse it at low cost

Direction:
- make the issue whether external structure usually overwhelms domestic variation, not whether domestic politics matters at all

### 4. Identity / legitimacy stem

Risk:
- can read like a contextual truism

Direction:
- sharpen whether legitimacy and meaning are causal drivers or mostly rhetorical overlays on material position

### 5. Political economy stem

Risk:
- currently too consensus-friendly for serious policy readers

Direction:
- sharpen whether production, finance, and dependence are central explanations or secondary context around strategic and diplomatic choices

### 6. Restraint / advantage stem

Risk:
- premise-loaded and normatively comfortable

Direction:
- sharpen the tradeoff between avoiding overextension and exploiting durable strategic openings

## Implementation rule

Do not do a broad rewrite.
For each of these, either:
- rewrite the current stem to sharpen the disagreement, or
- pair the current stem against a stronger opposing logic if that is easier within the current schema
