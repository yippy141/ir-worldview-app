01 Strip Atlas Stacked horizontal scales · baseline ↔ under-pressure markers per dimension
Why this reduces synthesis burden The reader gets every answer from a single visual. Each lane is its own scale; the open dot is where you started, the filled dot is where you ended up under module pressure, and a hairline links them. A glance gives you (1) overall shape — your baseline — (2) which dot pair has the longest link — what changed most — (3) which dot pair is shortest or absent — what stayed stable. The eye does the synthesis; the prose just confirms. AI sits as one quiet line below the chart, not a competing column.

03 Anchored Spread Baseline is the vertical anchor · lanes deviate left or right under pressure

Long bar = changed most; no bar = stayed stable; left vs right tells you which way you bent.
