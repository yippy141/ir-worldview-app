§2Where nearby archetypes part ways

Pairs that share most of their fingerprint. The split is what tells them apart.

Pair · A

Democratic Guardrailist vs Civic Risk Steward

The splitBoth center oversight. Guardrailist binds it to procedural legitimacy and public institutions; Steward binds it to specific publics and concentrated harms.
Pair · B

Strategic Competitor vs Industrial Statist

The splitBoth treat AI as state-level. Competitor reads it through capability edge; Statist reads it through instruments — procurement, subsidy, standards.
Pair · C

Open Ecosystem Builder vs Civic Risk Steward

The splitBoth worry about concentration. Builder defaults to release as the cure; Steward defaults to standing and remedy for affected groups.
Pair · D

Multilateral Coordinator vs Democratic Guardrailist

The splitBoth want rules. Coordinator looks outward to regimes and standards bodies; Guardrailist looks inward to domestic accountability.

§3How IR and AI cross-read

Three meeting points between the IR Foundation and the AI Compass. The two are related layers, not parallel assessments.

IR concept
Legitimacy of rule-setting

In IR, the question is whether legitimacy is procedural (right process) or substantive (right outcomes), and across whose regimes.

AI: Who has standing to audit a model, and on what authority?
IR concept
Security dilemma

In IR, competition can be self-fulfilling — defensive moves read as offensive, escalation follows.

AI: Pace pressure can be invoked or actual; the Field Guide names which is which.
IR concept
Political economy

In IR, the lens reads market dependence, coercion, and supply-chain leverage.

AI: Compute and data concentration are governance questions, not just market ones.

How each one avoids overloading the reader

Same content, different rhythms

01 · Reading Room

Vertical sequence. Each section is a ruled grid that the eye can scan as a single unit; the rules between cells do the structural work, so the reader never faces a paragraph longer than three sentences.

Best for the user who reads top-to-bottom and wants the page to feel like a printed spread.

02 · Two-Column Explainer

Persistent left rail. The reader can see the whole page's contents without scrolling and skip directly to the section they want. Futures is visibly the last item, pilled "optional," and demoted to dashed border.

Best for the user who arrives with a question — "where do Compass and IR meet?" — and wants to land in §3 immediately.
